Intro:

This lesson introduces key terms used in professional stage lighting and DMX control. These terms are essential for using the ETC Element 2 lighting console and understanding how fixtures are patched and operated.


Core Lighting Terms

Term Definition
DMX512 A digital communication protocol used to control stage lighting and effects. One DMX universe can control up to 512 parameters (channels).
Universe A group of 512 DMX channels. Most consoles, like the ETC Element 2, can handle multiple universes.
Channel A single controllable attribute of a lighting fixture, such as intensity, color, or pan/tilt.
Address The starting DMX channel assigned to a fixture, telling it where to listen on the DMX line.
Patch The process of assigning DMX addresses and channels to fixture numbers in your console.
Fixture A lighting instrument (e.g., LED, spotlight) that receives DMX signal for control.
Dimmer A unit that adjusts brightness of conventional lights, often controlled by one DMX channel.

Fixture & Control Types

Term Definition
Conventional Fixture A basic light like a PAR or fresnel, typically dimmed using one DMX channel.
Intelligent Fixture A fixture with multiple controllable attributes (e.g., color, position, effects).
LED Fixture A fixture that uses Light Emitting Diodes. Can use multiple channels (RGB, etc.).
Moving Light A fixture that can pan, tilt, and change color, often used for dynamic effects.
Gobo A metal or glass template placed inside a fixture to project patterns.
Pan/Tilt Movement axes of a moving light. Pan = horizontal, Tilt = vertical.

Console-Specific Terms (ETC Element 2)

Term Definition
Channel Number The virtual number in Element 2 that represents a fixture or dimmer.
Cue A recorded stage look that can be recalled in a show sequence.
Submaster (Sub) A fader that gives manual control of channels or cues on the console.
Group A saved set of channels that can be selected together.
Palette A saved value (like color or position) that can be reused across cues.
Patch Screen The console area where channels are assigned to DMX addresses.

Basic Equipment

Term Definition
DMX Cable A 3- or 5-pin cable used to transmit DMX512 signal. Looks like XLR but is different.
Terminator A plug with a resistor placed at the end of a DMX line to prevent signal interference.
Daisy Chain The method of connecting DMX fixtures in series using one continuous line.
Opto-splitter A device that splits and isolates DMX signals to send them to multiple outputs.

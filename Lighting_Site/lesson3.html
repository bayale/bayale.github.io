Intro:

This lesson introduces the main types of lighting instruments and their accessories. You’ll learn what each is best suited for, how to identify them, and how to expand their capabilities with common add-ons. Understanding the strengths and limitations of each fixture type is essential for effective lighting design, whether you’re working in theatre, dance, concerts, or events.

Conventional Fixtures

Ellipsoidal Reflector Spotlight (ERS)

Also known as Lekos or Source Four, ERS fixtures produce a highly focusable beam with sharp edges. Ideal for specials, pattern projection (using gobos), and precise area lighting. Shutters allow you to shape the beam, and accessories like irises and gobos expand versatility.

Fresnel

Fresnel fixtures create a soft-edged beam that can be adjusted from spot to flood. Great for area washes, blending light smoothly across the stage, and providing gentle back or top light. The distinctive lens gives them their name and unique quality.

PAR Can

PARs deliver a punchy, oval-shaped beam that’s not focusable. Used for strong washes, color effects, and concert lighting. Different lamp types (VNSP, NSP, MFL, WFL) change the beam spread.

Cyc Light

Designed to evenly illuminate large backdrops or cycloramas, cyc lights produce a wide, soft beam. Essential for creating smooth color gradients and backgrounds in theatre and dance.

Intelligent Fixtures

Moving Head

Moving heads can pan, tilt, change color, focus, and project patterns (gobos) all via remote control. They’re used for dynamic effects, quick repositioning, and complex cues in concerts, theatre, and events. Their versatility makes them a staple in modern lighting rigs.

Scanner

Scanners use a moving mirror to redirect a fixed light beam. They’re fast and precise, making them popular for club and concert lighting where rapid movement is needed. While less flexible than moving heads, they excel at rapid sweeps and chases.

Automated Profile

Automated profiles combine the focusability of ERS with the flexibility of moving heads. They often include framing shutters, zoom, and advanced effects, making them powerful tools for both theatre and events. Use them for precise specials, pattern projection, and complex scene changes.

Intelligent fixtures require DMX control and programming skills. They offer creative possibilities but need careful planning to avoid overwhelming a design.

LED Fixtures

RGB/RGBW PAR

These LED PARs allow flexible color mixing and are energy-efficient. Adding white (W) LEDs improves pastel and tint rendering. They’re used for washes, effects, and color-changing looks, and are popular for their versatility and low power consumption.

LED Cyc Light

Modern cyc lights use LEDs for even backdrop washes and instant color changes. They’re ideal for creating dynamic backgrounds and smooth gradients, and can be programmed for complex color effects.

Pixel Bar

Pixel bars contain multiple individually controllable sections, enabling chases, effects, and animations. They’re popular in concerts and installations for eye-catching visuals and creative stage looks.

LEDs are increasingly common due to their low power consumption, long life, and color flexibility. They’re transforming how designers approach color and effects.

Accessories

Barn Doors

Metal flaps that attach to Fresnels or PARs to control beam spill and shape the light. Useful for keeping light off unwanted areas.

Top Hat

Cylindrical accessories that reduce lens glare and help focus the beam, preventing light from shining into the audience’s eyes.

Gobo

Metal or glass patterns inserted into ERS fixtures to project textures, shapes, or images onto the stage or scenery.

Iris

A mechanical device that reduces the beam size in ERS fixtures, allowing for tighter spot effects and more precise control.

Color Frame

Holds gels or diffusion material in front of fixtures to change color or soften the beam. Essential for quick color changes and beam shaping.

Accessories expand the creative possibilities of your fixtures, allowing you to tailor the light to your exact needs.
